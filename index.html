How has Ruben Amorim fared in his first 35 Premier League games?

Despite patchy results, the Portuguese has now managed United for more league games than David Moyes. What do the stats say?

By Will Grimond

On Sunday, Ruben Amorim enjoyed maybe the biggest win in his stint as Manchester United manager, a 2-1 victory over a tired-looking Liverpool. But he also passed a significant milestone: managing 35 Premier League games, one more than David Moyes – United's shortest serving (permanent) manager in the post-Fergie era. He's hit a small-ish purple patch recently, but the data suggests his claim of coaching the "worst team maybe in the history of Manchester United" might ring true.

1. With just 40 league points, Ruben Amorim has had the worst start of any recent manager

Ruben Amorim has overseen poorer league performances than any of his predecessors, scraping together just 40 points in his first 35 games. No other post-Fergie manager has had such a torrid start – and Moyes paid the price despite better results, notching up 57 points in 34 games.

2. Amorim is the only recent United manager to have a negative goal difference

For all the criticism of José Mourinho's bus-parking, his impressive defensive record puts him level on goal difference with Louis Van Gaal. Meanwhile, Amorim's United has the unique accolade of conceding more league goals than they've scored, the only post-Fergie manager to do so.

3. Amorim has fewer points per game than his predecessors – although a better record than some against the 'Big Six'

Amorim's relatively low points per game tell a similar story, averaging just better than a draw in the league games he's managed. A small consolation comes from his record against top teams, which is better than some of his recent predecessors: David Moyes mustered a paltry 0.6 points per game against the so-called 'Big Six'.
